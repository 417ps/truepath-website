Our Story

From New Mexico roots to national innovation

TruePath Data Centers was born from a simple observation: while the world races toward an AI-powered future, the infrastructure to support it lags behind. Founded in 2024 by Michael Sanchez, a veteran contractor who successfully delivered over $400M in mission-critical infrastructure projects for Los Alamos National Labs, TruePath emerged from deep roots in New Mexico's technology corridor.

Michael witnessed firsthand how mission-critical facilities operate at the highest levels of reliability and security. He saw the gap between what hyperscalers need and what traditional data centers provide. Most importantly, he recognized that New Mexico was perfectly positioned to become a hub for next-generation data infrastructure with its abundant land, available power, and pro-business environment.

"Why New Mexico?"

People often ask why we chose the Southwest over Silicon Valley or Northern Virginia. The answer is simple: we go where others can't. While competitors fight over scraps of power in oversaturated markets, we identified a site with 600MW of potential power capacity in a Qualified Opportunity Zone location offering 20-year property tax abatement.

What started as a vision to bring world-class infrastructure to the Southwest has evolved into something bigger. By partnering with leading immersion cooling technology providers and assembling a team with over $20 billion in collective project experience, TruePath is reimagining what data centers can be.

Today, TruePath stands at the forefront of the AI infrastructure revolution, combining cutting-edge cooling technology with innovative financial structures to deliver facilities that are more efficient, more scalable, and more profitable than anything else in the market.
